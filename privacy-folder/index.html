PRIVACY POLICY

This Privacy Policy explains how UVATIME collects, uses, and protects your personal data when you visit our website or contact us about our services.

1. WHO WE ARE

UVATIME is a digital agency providing web development, Shopify, software, and mobile app services. For the purposes of data protection law (including the EU General Data Protection Regulation – GDPR, where applicable), the data controller may be:

  • UVATIME S.R.L., Brașov, Romania; and/or
  • UVATIME LLC, Jackson, Wyoming, USA;

depending on your location and the specific engagement.

2. DATA WE MAY COLLECT

We may collect the following categories of personal data:

  • Contact data: name, email address, phone number, company name, and any information you provide when contacting us.
  • Project-related data: information about your business, website, or app that you share for the purpose of working with us.
  • Technical data: IP address, device type, browser type, operating system, approximate location, pages visited, and similar analytics data.
  • Cookie & tracking data: information collected via cookies and similar technologies (see Section 6).

3. HOW WE USE YOUR DATA

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your messages, enquiries, and project requests;
  • To prepare proposals, quotes, and service agreements;
  • To manage and deliver projects and services you have requested from us;
  • To improve our website, services, and user experience;
  • To analyse website traffic and performance (e.g. via analytics tools);
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations (e.g. accounting, taxes).

4. LEGAL BASES (FOR EU / EEA USERS)

Where GDPR applies, we rely on the following legal bases to process your data:

  • Contractual necessity: to take steps at your request before entering into a contract and to perform a contract with you;
  • Legitimate interests: to improve our services, run and secure our website, and grow our business in a reasonable way;
  • Legal obligation: to comply with applicable laws (e.g. tax, accounting);
  • Consent: for certain cookies, newsletter subscriptions, or optional marketing communications (where applicable).

5. HOW LONG WE KEEP YOUR DATA

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, including satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. For example:

  • Basic contact and contract data may be kept for the duration of our collaboration and a reasonable period after;
  • Accounting and invoicing records are typically kept for the period required by law;
  • Analytics and technical data may be kept in aggregated or anonymised form where possible.

6. COOKIES & TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Remember your preferences and improve usability;
  • Analyse traffic and performance (e.g. which pages are visited and how often);
  • Support security and basic functionality.

When you first visit the website, you may see a cookie notice or banner explaining that we use cookies and linking back to this Privacy Policy. You can manage cookies through your browser settings and, where applicable, through our cookie banner.

7. SHARING YOUR DATA

We do not sell your personal data. We may share data with:

  • Service providers and subcontractors who help us deliver our services (e.g. hosting, email, project tools), under confidentiality obligations;
  • Professional advisers (lawyers, accountants, auditors) where necessary;
  • Authorities or regulators where required by law.

Where we use third-party tools (such as Shopify, analytics providers, or communication platforms), their own privacy policies also apply.

8. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

Because UVATIME operates from both the EU (Romania) and the US (Wyoming), and uses global service providers, your data may be transferred outside your country or outside the European Economic Area (EEA).

Where GDPR applies, we aim to ensure that such transfers are protected by appropriate safeguards, such as standard contractual clauses or equivalent measures, in line with applicable law.

9. YOUR RIGHTS (EU / EEA USERS)

Where GDPR applies, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access – to know what data we hold about you;
  • Right to rectification – to request correction of inaccurate data;
  • Right to erasure – to request deletion of your data, where applicable;
  • Right to restriction of processing – in certain circumstances;
  • Right to data portability – to receive your data in a structured, commonly used format;
  • Right to object – to certain types of processing, including where based on legitimate interests;
  • Right to withdraw consent – where processing is based on your consent.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below. We may need to verify your identity before responding.

10. SECURITY

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your data from unauthorised access, loss, misuse, or alteration. However, no transmission or storage system can be guaranteed to be 100% secure.

11. THIRD-PARTY WEBSITES

Our website may contain links to third-party websites (for example: Shopify stores, partners, tools). We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of those websites. We encourage you to read their privacy policies separately.

12. CHANGES TO THIS POLICY

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the “Last updated” date at the bottom of this page. Your continued use of the website after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
